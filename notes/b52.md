<details><summary>**Proof.** $E(X)$ Binomial$(n,p)$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X)&=\sum_{x=0}^nxp_X(x)=\sum_{x=1}^nxp_X(x)\tag{$0p_X(0)=0$}\\
  &=\sum_{x=1}^nx\binom nxp^kq^{n-x}=\sum_{x=1}^n\frac{xn!}{x!(n-x)!}p^xq^{n-x}\\
  &=\sum_{x=1}^n\frac{n(n-1)!}{(x-1)!(n-x)!}p^xq^{n-x}=\sum_{x=1}^nn\binom{n-1}{x-1}p^xq^{n-x}\\
  &=n\sum_{i=0}^{n-1}\binom{n-1}ip^{i+1}q^{n-i-1}\tag{$i=x-1$}\\
  &=np\sum_{i=0}^m\binom mip^iq^{m-i}=np(p+q)^m=np\tag{$m=n-1$}\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details><summary>**Proof.** $E(X)$ Geometric$(p)$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X)&=\sum_{n=0}^\infty np_X(n)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty np_X(n)=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty nq^n\tag{1}\\
  \therefore qE(X)&=p\sum_{n=0}^\infty nq^{n+1}=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty(n-1)q^n\tag{2}\\
  \therefore(1-q)E(X)&=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty(n-(n-1))q^n\tag{$1)-(2$}\\
  \therefore pE(X)&=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty (1-p)^n=p\frac{1-p}1-(1-p)=1-p\\
  \therefore E(X)&=\frac{1-p}p\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>

<details><summary>**Proof.** $E(X)$ Poisson$(\lambda)$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X)&=\sum_{n=0}^\infty np_X(n)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty np_X(n)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty n\frac{\lambda^n}{n!}e^{-\lambda}\\
  &=e^{-\lambda}\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{\lambda^n}{(n-1)!}=e^{-\lambda}\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{\lambda^{n+1}}{n!}=\lambda e^{-\lambda}\sum\frac{\lambda^n}{n!}\\
  &=\lambda e^{-\lambda}e^{-\lambda}=\lambda\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>

<details><summary>**Proof.** $V(X)$ Geometric$(p)$</summary>
find $E(X^2-X)=E(X(X-1))$
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X(X-1))&=\sum^\infty x(x-1)q^xp=p\sum x(x-1)q^x\\
  &=p\sum_{x=2}^\infty x(x-1)q^x=pq^2\sum_2 x(x-1)q^{x-2}\\
  &=pq^2\sum_2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}q^x=pq^2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}\sum_{x=2}^\infty q^x\\
  &=pq^2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}\left(\frac{q^2}{1-q}\right)\\
  \because\frac{q^2}{1-q}&=\frac{(1-q)^2+2q-1}{1-q}=1-q+\frac{2q}{1-q}-\frac1{1-q}\\
  \because\frac q{1-q}&=\frac{1-(1-q)}{1-q}=\frac1{1-q}-1\\
  \therefore\frac{q^2}{1-q}&=1-q+2\left(\frac1{1-q}-1\right)-\frac1{1-q}\\
  &=-1-q+\frac1{1-q}\\
  \therefore E(X(X-1))&=pq^2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}\left(-1-q+\frac1{1-q}\right)\\
  &=pq^22(1-q)^{-3}=2pq^2p^{-3}=2p^{-2}q^2\\
  &=E(X^2-X)
\end{align*}$$
find $V(X)=E(X^2+X-X)-\mu^2$
$$\begin{align*}
  V(X)&=E(X^2-X)+E(X)-\mu^2\\
  &=2\frac{q^2}{p^2}+\frac qp-\frac{q^2}{p^2}=\frac{q^2}{p^2}+\frac qp\\
  &=\frac{q^2+qp}{p^2}=\frac{q^2+q(1-q)}{p^2}=\frac q{p^2}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 2.7 Multivariate Distributions

### 2.7 Multivariate Discrete
<details class="definition" open><summary>Joint CDF $F_{XY}:\R^2\to[0,1]$ of $X$ and $Y$</summary>
$$F_{XY}(x,y)=P(X\le x,Y\le y)=P(\{X\le x\}\cap\{Y\le y\})$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Marginal CDF from joint CDF $F_{XY}$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  F_X(x)&=\lim_{y\to\infty}F_{XY}(x,y)\\
  F_X(y)&=\lim_{x\to\infty}F_{XY}(x,y)\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Marginal PMF from joint PMF</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  p_X(x)=\sum_yp_{XY}(x,y)=\sum_yP(X=x,Y=y)
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>$p_{XY}(x,y)=p^2q^{x+y}$ for $x,y\in\mathbb N$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  p_X(x)&=\sum_{y=0}^\infty p_{XY}(x,y)=\sum_y p^2q^xq^y\\
  &=p^2q^x\sum_yq^y=\frac{p^2q^x}{1-q}=pq^x\quad y\in\mathbb N\\
  F_{XY}(x,y)&=P(X\le x,Y\le y)=\sum_{i=0}^x\sum_{i=0}^yp_{XY}(i,j)=\sum_i^x\sum_j^yp^2q^iq^j\\
  &=p^2\sum_i^xq^i\sum_j^yq^j=p^2\cdot\frac{1-q^{x+1}}{1-q}\cdot\frac{1-q^{y+1}}{1-q}\\
  &=\left(1-q^{x+1}\right)\left(1-q^{y+1}\right)\quad x,y\in\mathbb N\\\
  P(X=Y)&=\sum_{i=0}^\infty p_{XY}(i,i)=\sum p^2q^{2i}=\frac{p^2}{1-q^2}\\
  &=\frac{p^2}{(1-q)(1+q)}=\frac p{1+q}=\frac p{2-p}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 2.6 1D Change of Variable
<details class="definition" open><summary>Discrete $Y=h(X)$</summary>
$$p_Y(y)=\sum_{x\in h^{-1}(y)}p_X(x)$$
where $h^{-1}(y)=\{x:h(x)=y\}$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Continuous $Y=h(X)$</summary>
- PDF method (only monotone and diffable $h$)
$$f_Y(y)=\frac{f_X(h^{-1}(y))}{|h'(h^{-1}(y))|}$$
- CDF method
  1. find 
  1. find $F_Y(y)$ wrt $F_X$
  2. find $f_Y(y)=\frac d{dy}F_Y(y)$ wrt $f_X$
  3. use def of $f_X(x)$ and find domain
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>$f_X(x)=2xe^{-x^2}$ for $x>0$, find $f_Y(y)$ where $Y=1/X$</summary>
- CDF
$$\begin{align*}
  \because F_Y(y)&=P(Y\le y)=P(1/X\le y)=P(X\ge 1/y)\\
  &=1-F_X(1/y)\\
  \therefore f_Y(y)&=\frac d{dy}(1-F_X(1/y))=-f_X(1/y)(1/y)'\\
  &=y^{-2}f_X\left(y^{-1}\right)=y^{-2}\left(2y^{-1}e^{-y^{-2}}\right)\\
  &=2y^{-3}e^{-y^{-2}}\quad\forall 1/y>0\implies\forall y>0\\
  f_Y(y)&=0\textsf{ otherwise}\\
\end{align*}$$

- PDF $\because Y=1/X=h(X)$ is monotone on $x>0$ i.e. supp$(X)$
$$\begin{align*}
  \because h^{-1}(y)&=y^{-1}\quad h'(y)=-y^{-2}\implies |h'(h^{-1}(y))|=y^2\\
  \therefore f_Y(y)&=\frac{f_X(h^{-1}(y))}{|h'(h^{-1}(y))|}=y^{-2}f_X\left(y^{-1}\right)=2y^{-3}e^{-y^{-2}}\quad\forall y>0\\
  f_Y(y)&=0\textsf{ otherwise}
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>$f_X(x)=\dfrac3{665}x^2$ for $4<x\le9$, and $Y=4-3X$</summary>
- find $P(Y\le-20)$
$$\begin{align*}
  P(Y\le-20)&=P(4-3X\le-20)=P(X\ge8)=\int_8^\infty f_X(x)\,dx\\
  &=\int_8^9\frac3{665}x^2\,dx=\frac3{665}\left[\frac{x^3}3\right]_8^9=\frac{9^3-8^3}{665}\\
\end{align*}$$
- find $f_Y(y)$ (CDF)
$$\begin{align*}
  \because F_Y(y)&=P(X\le y)=P(4-3X\le y)=P\left(X\ge\frac{4-y}3\right)\\
  &=1-P\left(X\le\frac{4-y}3\right)=1-F_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)\\
  \therefore f_Y(y)&=\frac d{dy}\left(1-F_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)\right)=-f_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)'\\
  &=\frac13f_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)=\frac13\frac3{665}\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)^2\quad 4<\frac{4-y}3\le9\\
  &=\frac{(4-y)^2}{5985}\quad -23<y\le-8\\
  f_Y(y)&=0\textsf{ otherwise}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 2.5 Cumulative Distribution Functions
<details class="definition" open><summary>CDF of $X$ is $F_X(x):\R\to[0,1]$</summary>
$$F_X(x)=P(X\le x)=\int_{-\infty}^x f_X(t)\,dt$$

| $P(a< X\le b)=F_X(b)-F_X(a)$
| $\displaystyle P(a\le X\le b)=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(a-1/n<x\le b)=F_X(b)-F_X(a^-)$



- $\forall x\in\R\enspace 0\le F_X(x)\le1$
- $x\le y\implies F_X(x)\le F_X(y)$ (nondecreases)
- $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}F_X(x)=1$
- $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to-\infty}F_X(x)=0$
</details>

## 2.4 Continuous Distributions
<details class="definition" open><summary>Continuous $\implies$ uncountably infinite</summary>
$X$ is **continuous** iff
\begin{align*}
  \forall x\in\R\enspace P(X=x)&=0\\
  \forall a<b\in\R\enspace P(X\in(a,b))&=P(a<X<b)
\end{align*}

(but $P(X\in\R)=1$)
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Probability Density Function (PDF) $f_X:\R\to\R$</summary>
$f_X:\R\to\R$ is a **density fn** iff
$$\forall x\in\R\enspace f_X(x)\ge0\textsf{ and }\int_{-\infty}^\infty f_X(x)\,dx=1$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Absolutely continuous</summary>
$X$ is **abs cont** iff
$$\exist f_X:\R\to\R\textsf{ st }P(a\le X\le b)=\int_a^bf_X(x)\,dx$$

- abs cont $\implies$ cont
- interval bounds does not matter
- $f_X(x)=F'_X(x)$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>use $N(0,10^2)$ to find $k$ for $f(x)=ke^{-\frac1{200}x^2}\quad x\ge10$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  \because 1&=\int_{10}^\infty ke^{-\frac1{200}x^2}\,dx=k\int_{10}^\infty e^{-\frac1{200}x^2}\,dx\\
  &=k10\sqrt{2\pi}\int_{10}^\infty\frac1{10\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12\left(\frac x{10}\right)^2}\,dx\\
  &=k10\sqrt{2\pi}P(X>10)\quad\textsf{for }X\sim N(0,10^2)\\
  \because P(X>10)&=\Phi(-z)=\Phi\left(-\frac{10-\mu}\sigma\right)=\Phi\left(-\frac{10-0}{10}\right)\\
  &=\Phi(-1)\\
  \therefore1&=k10\sqrt{2\pi}P(X>10)=10\sqrt{2\pi}k\Phi(-1)\\
  \therefore k&=\frac1{10\sqrt{2\pi}\Phi(-1)}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 2.3 Discrete Distribution
<details class="definition" open><summary>Discrete $X$</summary>
$X$ is **discrete** iff
$$\sum_{x\in\mathbb R}P(X=x)=1$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Probability Mass Function (PMF) of $X$ is $p_X(x):\R\to[0,1]$</summary>
$$p_X(x)=P(X=x)=P(\{s\in S:X(s)=x\})$$

- $\displaystyle \sum_{x\in\textrm{im}X}p_X(x)=1$
- PMF $\to$ CDF: $\displaystyle F_X(x)=P(X\le x)=\sum_{x_i\le x}p_X(x_i)$
</details>

## 2.2 Distribution of RV
<details class="definition" open><summary>Distribution: $P(X\in B)\enspace\forall B\sube\mathbb R$</summary>
(discrete) if $S=\{s_i\}\quad P(s_i)=p_i\quad X(s_i)=x_i$ then
\begin{align*}
  P(X\in B\sube\mathbb R)&=P(\{s:\in S:X(s)\in B\})\\

</details>

# 1 Probability Model

<details class="definition" open><summary></summary>
- **Outcome**: simplest result of experiment
- **Sample Space $S$**: set of all outcomes
- **Event**: subset of $S$ i.e. set of outcomes
- **Probability Measure** $P:\textsf{event}\to[0,1]$ with the axioms
</details>

<details class="theorem" open><summary>Probability Axioms</summary>
1. $0\leqslant P(A)\leqslant1\quad\forall A\sube S$
2. $P(S)=1$
3. $\{A_i\}_{i=1}^\infty$ disjoint $\displaystyle\implies P\left(\bigcup A_i\right)=\sum P(A_i)$
</details>

<details class="definition" open><summary>Probability Model</summary>
$$(S,\{A_i\},P)$$

where
: $S$ is the **sample space**
: $\{A_i\}$ is the **set of events**
: $P$ is probability **measure**

</details>

## Set/Event Operations

> use $A^c$ for $\overline A$

<details class="theorem" open><summary>$A\cap B\equiv AB\quad A\cup B$</summary>
- commutative, associative, distributive
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>$\overline A=\{s:s\not\in A\}$ **Complement**</summary>
- $A\cap \overline A=\varnothing\quad A\cup \overline A=S\quad \overline S=\varnothing$
- $\overline{A\cap B}=\overline A\cup \overline B\quad \overline{A\cup B}=\overline A\cap \overline B$
</details>

## 1.3 Properties of Probability Models

<details class="theorem" open><summary>Law of Total Probability</summary>
let $\{A_i\}$ be a paritition of $S$, for any $B\sube S$
$$P(B)=\sum P(A_i\cap B)$$

$\implies\sum P(A_i)=1$<br>
$\implies P(A)+P(\overline A)=P(S)=1$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open><summary>Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion</summary>
$$P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)$$

### Combinatorics
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Mulitplication Principle</summary>
with $\{a_1,\cdots,a_m\}$ and $\{b_1,\cdots,b_n\}$<br>
there are $mn$ possible **ordered pairs** of $(a_i,b_j)$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Permutation</summary>
$$P^n_k=\frac{n!}{(n-k)!}$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Binomial Combination (divide into 2 groups)</summary>
$$\binom nk=\binom n{n-k}=\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Multinomial Combination (divide into $l$ groups)</summary>
for $\sum k_i=n$

\begin{align*}
  \binom n{k_1k_2\cdots k_l}&=\binom n{k_1}\binom{n-k_1}{k_2}\binom{n-k_1-k_2}{k_3}\cdots\binom{k_l}{k_l}\\
  &=\frac{n!}{k_1!k_2!\cdots k_l!}\\
\end{align*}

</details>

: $S$: distribute any 52 cards to 4 people

</details>

## 1.5 Conditional Probability & Indep

### 1.5.1 Conditional Probability

<details class="definition" open><summary>given events $A,B$ st $P(B)>0$</summary>
$$P(A|B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}=\frac{|A\cap B|}{|B|}$$

$\iff P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B|A)$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>$P(A|BC)=P(A|B,C)=P(A|B\cap C)$</summary>
$$P(A|BC)=\frac{P(A\cap B\cap C)}{P(B\cap C)}$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Law of Total Probability</summary>
given $\{A_i\}$ as **partition** of $S$
$$\begin{align*}
  \forall B\enspace P(B)&=\sum P(A_i)P(B|A_i)\\
  &=P(A)P(B|A)+P(\overline A)P(B|\overline A)
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Bayes' Theorem</summary>
$$P(A|B)=\frac{P(A)}{P(B)}P(B|A)=\frac{P(A)}{P(A)P(B|A)+P(\overline A)P(B|\overline A)}P(B|A)$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>Draw Veinn to Save Life</summary>
given
: $P(A\cup B)\quad P(A\cap B)$
: $E=(A\cap\overline B)\cup(\overline A\cap B)$

find $P(E|A\cup B)$

1. $\displaystyle P(E|(A\cup B))=\frac{P(E\cap(A\cup B))}{P(A\cup B)}$
2. draw veinn to see $E\sube A\cup B$ and $P(E)=P(A\cup B)-P(A\cap B)$
3. $\displaystyle\therefore P(E|(A\cup B))=\frac{P(A\cup B)-P(A\cap B)}{P(A\cup B)}$

</details>

### 1.5.2 Indep

<details class="definition" open><summary>(Mutual) Indep wrt $P$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  A\perp B&\iff P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)\\
  &\iff P(A|B)=P(A)\\
  (\perp A_i)&\iff\forall B\sube A\enspace P(\cap B_i)=\prod P(B_i)
\end{align*}$$

(a) $\implies A\perp\overline B$
(b) $\implies \overline A\perp\overline B$
</details>

<details class="definition" open><summary>Pairwise Indep wrt $P$</summary>
$$\forall i\ne j\enspace P(A_i\cap A_j)=P(A_i)P(A_j)$$
</details>

### Conditional Indep
<details class="definition" open><summary>$A$ and $B$ are conditionally indep wrt $P_C$ iff</summary>
$$P(A\cap B|C)=P(A|C)P(B|C)$$
</details>

## 1.6 Continuity

<details class="definition" open><summary></summary>
| increases | decreases |
|-:|:-|
| $A_1\sube A_2\sube\cdots$ and $\cup A_n=A$ | $A_1\supe A_2\supe\cdots$ and $\cap A_n=A$ |
| $\{A_n\}\nearrow A$ | $\{A_n\}\searrow A$ |
| $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}A_n=A$ | $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}A_n=A$

\begin{align*}
  \lim_{n\to\infty}A_n=A\implies\lim_{n\to\infty}P(A_n)&=P(A)\\
\end{align*}
</details>

<details class="theorem" open><summary></summary>
\begin{align*}
  \{A_n\}\searrow A&\implies\{\overline{A_n}\}\nearrow\overline A\\
  \lim_{n\to\infty}P(\cup\overline{A_n})&=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(\overline{\cap A_n})
\end{align*}
</details>

<details class="example"><summary>Geometric series</summary>
given $S=\mathbb Z^+\quad\forall s\in S\enspace P(s)=2^{-s}$<br>
find $P(A=\{5,6,7,8,\cdots\})$
\begin{align*}
\textsf{let }A_n&=\{5,6,\cdots,n\}\\
\therefore\{A_n\}&\nearrow A\\
\therefore P(A)&=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(A_n)\\
  &=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(\{5,6,\cdots,n\})\\
  &=\lim_{n\to\infty}(P(5)+P(6)+\cdots+P(n))\\
  &=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=5}^n P(i)=\sum_{i=5}^\infty\frac 1{2^i}=\frac{2^{-5}}{1-1/2}=2^{-4}\\
\end{align*}
</details>

<details class="example"><summary>Make $A_n$ to find $P(A)$</summary>
given $\displaystyle P\left(\left[0,\frac8{4+n}\right]\right)=\frac{2+e^{-n}}6$<br>
find $P(\{0\})$

\begin{align*}
  \textsf{let }A_n&=\left[0,\frac8{4+n}\right]\\
  \therefore A_n&\searrow\{0\}\\
  \therefore P(\{0\})&=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(A_n)\\
  &=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{2+e^{-n}}6=\frac13
\end{align*}
</details>
